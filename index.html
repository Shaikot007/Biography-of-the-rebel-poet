Kazi Nazrul Islam

"The Rebel Poet"

Nazrul produced a large body of poetry and music with themes that included religious devotion and spiritual rebellion against fascism and oppression. Nazrul's activism for political and social justice earned him the title of "Rebel Poet".

Here's a time line of Kazi Nazrul Islam's life:

  • 1899 - He was born.
  • 1908 - His father was death.
  • 1910 - Nazrul left the troupe and enrolled at the Searsole Raj High School in Raniganj.
  • 1914 - Nazrul studied in the Darirampur School (now Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University) in Trishal, Mymensingh District.
  • 1917 - He enlisted in the British Indian Army at the age of eighteen.
  • 1919 - His first prose work, "Baunduler Atmakahini" ("Life of a Vagabond") and poem "Mukti" "মুক্তি" ("Freedom") were published.
  • 1922 - Nazrul reached the peak of fame with Bidrohi (The Rebel), which remains his most famous work, winning the admiration of India's literary classes for his description of a rebel as someone whose impact is fierce and ruthless even as his spirit is deep.
  • 1923 - He was transferred from the jail in Alipore to Hooghly in Kolkata.
  • 1930 - His book Pralayshikha was banned and he faced charges of sedition.
  • 1936 - The film Vidyapati (Master of Knowledge) was produced based on his recorded play.
  • 1939 - Nazrul's wife Pramila fell seriously ill and was paralysed from the waist down.
  • 1942 - He was admitted to a mental asylum.
  • 1952 - He was transferred to a mental hospital in Ranchi.
  • 1972 - The newly independent nation of Bangladesh brought Nazrul to live in Dhaka with the consent of the Government of India.
  • 1976 - Nazrul succumbed to his long-standing ailments.

"Bidrohi (The Rebel)"

I am the unutterable grief,

I am the trembling first touch of the virgin,

I am the throbbing tenderness of her first stolen kiss.

I am the fleeting glance of the veiled beloved,

I am her constant surreptitious gaze...

I am the burning volcano in the bosom of the earth,

I am the wildfire of the woods,

I am Hell's mad terrific sea of wrath!

I ride on the wings of lightning with joy and profundity,

I scatter misery and fear all around,

I bring earthquakes on this world! "(8th stanza)"

I am the rebel eternal,

I raise my head beyond this world,

High, ever erect and alone!

His famous poem translation by Kabir Choudhary
